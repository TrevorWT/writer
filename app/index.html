How Writer works

The five ideas that make everything else make sense.

Your stories are plain files.
A story is a folder of markdown - readable in any editor, safe forever, never locked in. Pick any folder as your library and start.
Zoom instead of scroll.
Panels are your story's pieces. Click one to dive in, or Ctrl+scroll up over it. Ctrl+scroll down (or Esc, or the arrow by the title) backs out. Leaves are scenes - add a panel inside a scene and it becomes a chapter on its own.
Plan and write in the same place.
Text on a chapter or part is planning - it stays out of your exported manuscript. Text in a scene is the book. The Notes tab on the right edge holds side notes for any section.
Tag your world as you write.
Select a character's name, right-click, Tag. Every mention everywhere becomes a link to their page, which lists everywhere they appear. Same for places, items, anything.
You can't lose work.
Everything autosaves. Ctrl+Z undoes moves and deletes. The clock icon keeps snapshots and automatic backups of anything overwritten - restore with one click.

Every shortcut and gesture: Settings > Hotkeys. Reopen this guide anytime with the ? button on the left rail.
